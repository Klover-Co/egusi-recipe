EGUSI SOUP

A classic Nigerian dish, Egusi soup is a rich and flavorful soup made with ground melon seeds, meat, fish, and leafy greens.
The recipe below uses the popular "fried method" for a rich, textured result.

Prep time Cook time Servings Difficulty
30 minutes 40 minutes 6 Medium

Ingredients

  • 4 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 300ml red palm oil
  • 2 kg assorted meat (beef, shaki/tripe, goat meat, etc.), cooked with stock reserved
  • 1 medium-sized stock fish head, soaked
  • 1/2 cup ground crayfish1/2 cup ground crayfish
  • 2 cups washed bitter leaves or chopped spinach/pumpkin leaves
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3-4 seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • 3-4 scotch bonnet peppers, blended (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp dawadawa or iru (fermented locust beans, optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions (Fried Method)

  1. Prepare the Egusi Paste: In a bowl, mix the ground egusi seeds with a little water or some of the reserved
    meat stock to form a thick, slightly lumpy paste. Set aside.
  2. Fry the Egusi: In a large pot, heat the palm oil over medium heat. Do not let the oil bleach. Add the chopped onions and saut√©
    until translucent. Add the egusi paste in small scoops to the oil. Fry for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent
    burning, until the egusi is fried and clumpy and the oil has separated from the mixture.
  3. Simmer the Soup Base: Gradually add the reserved meat and fish stock to the fried egusi, stirring well to combine.
    Add the cooked meats, stockfish, blended peppers, ground crayfish, dawadawa (if using), and seasoning cubes. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  4. Cook and Season: Cover the pot and allow it to simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    The egusi needs to cook properly to prevent an upset stomach. Taste and adjust seasoning with more salt or seasoning cubes if needed.
  5. Add Vegetables and Serve: Add the chopped leafy greens (spinach, pumpkin leaves, or bitter leaves). Stir gently to combine.
    Taste for salt and adjust seasoning as needed. Cook for another 3 to 5 minutes, then remove from the heat.

Serving Suggestions

Egusi soup is traditionally served with a West African swallow such as eba (garri), pounded yam, fufu, or semovita.
It also pairs well with white rice or boiled plantains for a comforting, filling meal.
